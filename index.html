The Career of Malik Nabers

Malik Nabers (born July 28, 2003) is an American professional football wide receiver for the New York Giants of the NFL. He played college football for the LSU Tigers and is entering his second year with the Giants. He’s a volume-dominant receiver with game-breaking upside.

  • New York Giants (official)
  • LSU Football

College Career

As a true freshman at LSU in 2021, Nabers played in 11 games with six starts and had 28 receptions for 417 yards and four touchdowns. He returned to LSU as a starter in 2022; Nabers was named MVP of the 2023 Citrus Bowl with nine catches for 163 yards and a touchdown pass to Jayden Daniels. In 2023 he posted 13 receptions for 239 yards and two TDs vs. Mississippi State and finished as a unanimous All-American, leading the FBS with 1,569 receiving yards. He declared for the 2024 NFL Draft and finished as LSU’s all-time leader in receiving yards (3,003).

Rookie Year

Drafted sixth overall by the Giants in 2024, Nabers set franchise marks and earned a 2025 Pro Bowl selection. With a new QB in year two, expectations are even higher for continued growth.

Records Set

  1. Most receptions by a rookie WR in a single season: 109
  2. Youngest WR with multiple touchdowns in a game: 21 years and 56 days
  3. Fastest to reach 100 career receptions: 14 games (tied with Odell Beckham Jr.)
  4. Most targets by a rookie in a season: 170
  5. Most receptions at home by a rookie in a season: 58
